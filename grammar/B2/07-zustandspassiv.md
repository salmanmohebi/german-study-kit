## Erklärung

German distinguishes between two passive types:

| Typ | Form | Bedeutung |
|---|---|---|
| **Vorgangspassiv** | werden + Partizip II | the **process** ("X is being done") |
| **Zustandspassiv** | **sein + Partizip II** | the **state / result** ("X is in the done condition") |

Compare:

> *Die Tür **wird** geschlossen.* — Someone is closing the door right now.
> *Die Tür **ist** geschlossen.* — The door is in a closed state.

In English, both often translate to "is closed", but German makes the distinction explicit. Use Zustandspassiv when describing the **result** of a completed action.

## Sätze

- Die Tür {{c1::ist}} geschlossen. | The door is closed.
- Das Geschäft {{c1::ist}} schon geöffnet. | The shop is already open.
- Der Brief {{c1::ist}} noch nicht geschrieben. | The letter isn't written yet.
- Das Auto {{c1::ist}} repariert. | The car is repaired.
- Die Aufgabe {{c1::ist}} bereits erledigt. | The task is already done.
- Das Hotel {{c1::ist}} komplett gebucht. | The hotel is completely booked.
- Mein Computer {{c1::ist}} kaputt. | My computer is broken.
- Die Tickets {{c1::sind}} ausverkauft. | The tickets are sold out.
- Der Kuchen {{c1::ist}} schon gebacken. | The cake is already baked.
- Das Fenster {{c1::war}} offen. | The window was open.
- Die Briefe {{c1::sind}} bereits abgeschickt. | The letters are already sent.
- Der Tisch {{c1::ist}} schon gedeckt. | The table is already set.
- Die Wäsche {{c1::war}} schon gewaschen. | The laundry was already washed.
- Das Buch {{c1::ist}} ausgeliehen. | The book is checked out.
- Mein Auto {{c1::war}} versperrt. | My car was locked.
- Die Sache {{c1::ist}} geklärt. | The matter is resolved.
- Das Spiel {{c1::ist}} verloren. | The game is lost.
- Die Stelle {{c1::ist}} noch besetzt. | The position is still filled.
- Das Fest {{c1::war}} schon vorbei. | The party was already over.
- Die Pläne {{c1::sind}} fertig. | The plans are finished.
