## Erklärung

The **Plusquamperfekt** is the "past in the past" — used when one past event happened **before** another past event.

> **hatte / war + Partizip II**

The same haben/sein rules apply as for Perfekt. Most often paired with **nachdem**, **bevor**, **als** to show the order of events.

> *Nachdem ich gegessen **hatte**, ging ich joggen.* (eating happened first)
> *Sie war schon **eingeschlafen**, als ich nach Hause kam.* (sleeping started before arriving)

In English: *I had eaten…*, *She had fallen asleep…*

## Sätze

- Ich {{c1::hatte}} schon gegessen, als er kam. | I had already eaten when he came.
- Nachdem wir angekommen {{c1::waren}}, gingen wir essen. | After we had arrived, we went to eat.
- Sie {{c1::hatte}} das Buch schon gelesen, bevor sie den Film sah. | She had already read the book before she saw the film.
- Wir {{c1::waren}} müde, weil wir lange gearbeitet hatten. | We were tired because we had worked a long time.
- Bevor er anrief, {{c1::hatte}} ich schon geschlafen. | Before he called, I had already slept.
- Er {{c1::hatte}} viel gelernt, deshalb bestand er die Prüfung. | He had studied a lot, that's why he passed the exam.
- Nachdem die Kinder gegessen {{c1::hatten}}, gingen sie ins Bett. | After the children had eaten, they went to bed.
- Ich {{c1::hatte}} ihn nie zuvor gesehen. | I had never seen him before.
- Sie {{c1::war}} schon weg, als ich ankam. | She had already left when I arrived.
- Wir {{c1::hatten}} alles geplant, aber das Wetter war schlecht. | We had planned everything, but the weather was bad.
- Nachdem wir angerufen {{c1::hatten}}, kam das Taxi. | After we had called, the taxi came.
- Er sagte, dass er das Buch gelesen {{c1::hatte}}. | He said that he had read the book.
- Bevor sie sprach, {{c1::hatte}} sie lange überlegt. | Before she spoke, she had thought for a long time.
- Ich {{c1::war}} noch nie in Berlin gewesen. | I had never been to Berlin.
- Nachdem das Konzert begonnen {{c1::hatte}}, kamen wir an. | After the concert had started, we arrived.
- Sie {{c1::war}} schon eingeschlafen, als ich nach Hause kam. | She had already fallen asleep when I came home.
- Bevor du kamst, {{c1::hatte}} ich schon angefangen. | Before you came, I had already started.
- Wir {{c1::waren}} schon abgereist, als das Hotel anrief. | We had already left when the hotel called.
- Er {{c1::hatte}} mich nicht erkannt. | He hadn't recognized me.
- Nachdem wir uns kennengelernt {{c1::hatten}}, schrieben wir uns oft. | After we had met, we often wrote to each other.
