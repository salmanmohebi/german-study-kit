## Erklärung

In spoken German, the past tense is normally **Perfekt**. But for **sein** and **haben** (and the modal verbs), Germans almost always use the **Präteritum** — even in conversation. So memorize these forms now.

| Person | sein | haben |
|---|---|---|
| ich | war | hatte |
| du | warst | hattest |
| er / sie / es | war | hatte |
| wir | waren | hatten |
| ihr | wart | hattet |
| sie / Sie | waren | hatten |

Common pattern: *Wo **warst** du? — Ich **war** zu Hause.* / *Ich **hatte** keine Zeit.*

## Sätze

- Ich {{c1::war}} gestern sehr müde. | I was very tired yesterday.
- Du {{c1::warst}} den ganzen Tag zu Hause. | You were home all day.
- Er {{c1::war}} in der Schule. | He was at school.
- Wir {{c1::waren}} gestern im Kino. | We were at the cinema yesterday.
- Ihr {{c1::wart}} sehr nett zu mir. | You all were very nice to me.
- Sie {{c1::waren}} im Urlaub in Spanien. | They were on vacation in Spain.
- Ich {{c1::hatte}} keine Zeit. | I had no time.
- Du {{c1::hattest}} eine sehr gute Idee. | You had a very good idea.
- Sie {{c1::hatte}} großen Hunger. | She was very hungry.
- Wir {{c1::hatten}} viel Spaß auf der Party. | We had a lot of fun at the party.
- {{c1::Hattet}} ihr ein Problem mit dem Auto? | Did you (pl.) have a problem with the car?
- Sie {{c1::hatten}} damals keinen Hund. | They had no dog back then.
- Wir {{c1::waren}} damals jung. | We were young at that time.
- Sie {{c1::hatte}} keinen Schlüssel dabei. | She didn't have her key with her.
- Du {{c1::warst}} sehr leise. | You were very quiet.
- Ich {{c1::hatte}} große Angst. | I was very afraid.
- Er {{c1::war}} nicht zu Hause. | He was not at home.
- Wir {{c1::hatten}} viele Freunde. | We had many friends.
- {{c1::Wart}} ihr im Restaurant? | Were you (pl.) at the restaurant?
- Sie {{c1::waren}} sehr froh. | They were very happy.
